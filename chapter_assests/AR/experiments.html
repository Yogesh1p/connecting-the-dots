Let's Test This.

Don't just read the ideas — probe them. Each experiment below is designed to break an assumption and show you what's actually happening under the hood.

What these experiments are

Every section of Chapter 1 builds an intuition. These experiments are designed to stress-test those intuitions — to ask: is this actually doing what I think it is?

The experiments run in Google Colab. No setup required. The code is minimal and annotated — the goal is not to build a system but to see something clearly.

Exp 02 Transformer Information Retrieval — does x⁽ᴸ⁾ drift toward the answer as layers accumulate?

Model: Qwen 2.5 3B · 36 layers · T4 GPU. The story ends mid-sentence: "After reviewing the evidence, the police concluded that the murderer was". John and Sarah have airtight alibis. Michael was the only one home. We read x⁽ˡ⁾ at the last token position across all 36 layers and measure cos(x⁽ˡ⁾, e_name) for each suspect.

Does the residual stream build toward the correct answer — Michael — gradually across layers, or does the answer only appear at the very top? And what are John and Sarah's curves doing while Michael's rises?

Exp 03 Suspect Alignment as Story Unfolds — does x⁽ᴸ⁾ update as each piece of evidence arrives?

Model: Qwen 2.5 1.5B · T4 GPU. The same three suspects. This time we feed the story incrementally — one checkpoint at a time — and read x⁽ᴸ⁾ at the final layer after each new piece of evidence. John gets an alibi. Sarah gets an alibi. Michael is placed at the scene.

Does cos(x⁽ᴸ⁾, e_Michael) rise at the moment Michael is implicated, and stay there? Do John and Sarah's curves fall at the moment their alibis are given? Is the hidden state tracking the story the way a reader would?

Run it and share what you find

Open the notebook, run all cells, and watch the three curves across checkpoints. Does Michael's curve rise sharply after he's placed at the scene, or does the model take longer to commit? Do the alibis immediately suppress John and Sarah, or do their scores lag? The result will tell you whether the residual stream is doing real-time inference or just pattern matching at the end.

Exp 04 Knight-Knave Logic Puzzle — does the model resolve a logical constraint across layers?

Model: Qwen 2.5 7B · T4 GPU. Puzzle: Aiken says Benny never tells the truth. Aiken says Candy always lies. Dueet says Candy is a knave and I am a knave. The logical answer is: Benny is a knight. We track cos(x⁽ˡ⁾, e_knight) and cos(x⁽ˡ⁾, e_knave) across all 28 layers.

Does the knight curve separate from the knave curve gradually — the model slowly resolving the constraint — or does it snap into place late? At what layer does the model effectively commit to the answer?

Run it and share what you find

Open the notebook and plot both curves. Look for the layer where they cross. Is the separation clean or noisy? Does the knave curve start high and fall, or never meaningfully rise? The shape of the crossing tells you something about how and when the model resolves logical inference — whether it reasons across many layers or compresses the decision into a few.
